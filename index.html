Black Hole

A black hole is an incredibly dense object in space formed when a massive star collapses under its own gravity. Its gravitational pull is so strong that nothing, not even light can escape once it crosses a boundary called the event horizon. At the center lies a point known as a singularity, where matter is compressed to extremely high density. Black holes were first predicted by Albert Einstein’s theory of general relativity.
